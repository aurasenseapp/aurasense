Learning to see the aura
A short practice course, drawn from methods taught openly for over a century — including those popularised by writers like Ted Andrews — retold here in Aurasense's own words. Everyone senses energy; seeing it is mostly a matter of un-learning how hard we normally look.
1 · Soften the eyes. Vision for auras is peripheral, not direct. Practise letting your gaze rest near a thing rather than on it — the way you look at stars at night, where the faint ones appear only off-centre.
2 · Begin with your own hand. Hold it against a plain, softly lit background — white or pale grey works best. Spread the fingers slightly. Rest your soft gaze just past the fingertips and wait, without straining. Blinking is fine.
3 · The first band. Most people first see a thin, almost transparent outline hugging the skin, a few millimetres wide — sometimes like heat-shimmer. This is usually called the etheric layer. Seeing it may take seconds or several sittings. Both are normal.
4 · Let colour arrive; never chase it. With practice the band widens and colour impressions come — sometimes seen, sometimes simply known, like sensing a person’s mood from across a room. Both count. Many strong aura-readers feel colour rather than see it.
5 · What the colours tend to mean. Traditions vary, but there is broad agreement: rose for love and compassion · orange-red for vitality and drive · gold-yellow for clarity and joy · green for healing and growth · blue for calm and truth-speaking · violet for intuition and the spiritual · dimmed or greyed regions for tiredness or held tension. Your own Aurasense colours use this same language.
6 · People, plants, places. Trees against dawn or dusk sky are wonderful practice — their fields are steady and generous. Then a friend against a plain wall. Then, gently, places themselves: thresholds, old rooms, street corners. Notice before naming.
7 · The ethic. An aura is as private as a diary. Look at others’ fields the way you would hope to be looked at — with warmth, without judgement, and never to catalogue their weather. What you practise here is compassion with its eyes open.
Now use it here. Every colour that enters your My Aura — from offerings, questionnaires, and the Soul Search — follows this same colour language. You are learning to read yourself first. Others’ auras will follow naturally.
